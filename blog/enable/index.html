Enable embarked on a comprehensive redesign of its entire B2B application with the ambitious goal of becoming the leading restaurant management platform. This project aims to pinpoint usability issues and prioritize critical features for the next iteration of our product.



Improving the UX and UI of our platform necessitates a deep understanding of user personas. Given Enable's expansive oversight of restaurant operations, encompassing call centers, branches, and restaurant managers, our primary objective is to intricately grasp the needs of each user and optimize their journey throughout the platform.



In my role, I had the opportunity to focus on the call center, where both call center agents and operational teams are pivotal users.



I had the privilege of working under the guidance of one of the top senior product designers, Sara Alshimi. Throughout the process, she served as an invaluable mentor, imparting a wealth of knowledge and expertise that has greatly influenced my growth.

The Process



Our process was not linear but the most important thing is being flexible to discover more, understandable and curious.

 


 

 


 

 

Understanding the problems



Before Enable even hired a product design team, a beta release of the new platform had been implemented based on blue-sky concepts executed by an external consultancy. These mockups were created without any usability testing and had little consideration for the technical and product limitations on the scope of work.

I conducted research interviews with our primary users (Call Center agents and operation team) to uncover any pain points that they were experiencing with the beta release.

My research encompassed:

- Understanding the user goals and needs.

- Uncovering pain points with the existing user journey.

- Determining the success of the tasks measured.



 

 


 

 

Gathering Insights



After collecting the recordings from the user interviews, I conducted affinity mapping with my teammate to synthesize the pains identified. We grouped these problems under common themes and features in the platform.

 


 


 

 


 

 

Sketching Solutions



I usually prefer using pen and paper for sketching my solutions and design before start working on the UI because it always gives me space fore creativity and exposing all the ideas as well as discussing them freely with my team.

Based on the above problems identified, I worked towards addressing these pains by coming up with potential solutions depending on the problem I am facing.



 

 


 

 

Validation of the design



After creating the UI, I conducted usability testing sessions with our primary users to validate whether the new designs would solve their problems.

I wrote a script including a scenario asking the user to do the task, I observed how they interacted with the prototype. The usability session always gives me insights about what needs to be done and what needs to be focused on more.

Then came the design process.

Developing the design



I created my high-fidelity mockups in Figma and then imported them into Zeplin to allow the engineers to inspect the file and export the HTML and CSS code.

I worked closely with the Front End team to specify any missing interactions not covered in the high-fidelity mockups.

I conducted a UX review of each front-end ticket implemented to ensure it aligned with the designs before it went live.
